Remembering 'Abdu'l-Bahá’s Time in San Francisco, 1912

Rented Residence of 'Abdu'l-Bahá

According to Ramona Allen in Memories of 'Abdu'l-Bahá, p. 34,

“He had rented a lovely home at 1815 California Street, which had been selected for Him and His entourage by Helen [Goodall] and Ella [Cooper]. “

In a letter sent to the Spiritual Assembly of the Bahá’ís of San Francisco, January 25, 1979 from Inez Pischel Ettelson, granddaughter of the Pischels from whom the home had been rented, the following information is shared:

“…in regard to what I recall of the interior of my grandmother's home, at 1915 [sic] California Street. The front hall was a large square room denominated by a large tile stove -white tile with colored tile trim of the type seen in all the northern European countries. It gave off a lovely heat! The dining room and the living room opened off of this hall. On the second floor there were three bedrooms, master bedroom overlooked a good sized garden -as did the living room below. The kitchen, etc. were in the basement, food and dishes coming up and down to the dining room via a 'dumb waiter' manipulated by means of a rope. On the fourth floor were other bedrooms.

This is not much of a summary, but it is about alI I remember clearly. At one time the living room connected with a conservatory which in turn connected with 1817 California Street where my parents lived but I do not recall when or why it was torn down. Probably after the death of my grandmother.

I also do not recall just how the house happened to be up for rental. But probably it so happened that my grandparents were in Europe at the time. My parents, my sister and myself were in Europe most of 1912 so I was probably not living at home at the time the place was occupied by 'Abdu'I-Baha.

I'm sorry not to be of more help to you, but like most young people I was no doubt very involved with my own affairs -and not very interested in the rest of the world!

Sincerely,

Inez P. Ettelson”

According to "ABDUL BAHA: A call on the Persian Prophet Who Has Been Making a Visit to San Francisco, Carrying the Message of Universal Harmony" article by John D. Barry, printed in the San Francisco Bulletin, 10/12/12, p 6, col. 2-4, this home was the location of several interviews of the press and small gatherings with visitors who came to meet with 'Abdu'I-Baha.

“When I entered the comfortable home at 1815 California street, where the prophet was staying, I saw a great many people. They were sitting about to the side hall and in the rooms that radiated into the distance.”

“Abdul Baha Suffrage Advocate, Sex Equality Need for Progress" article in the San Francisco Examiner, 10/4/12, p. 3, col.2-3 reported

“Surrounded by his Persian secretaries, Abdul Baha Abbas, one of the great leaders of modern times and spiritual advisor to fifty million followers, sat in an armchair at 1815 California street last night, discussing American manners and ideals, suffrage and women's fashions, and told of the most wonderful things he had found in America.”
