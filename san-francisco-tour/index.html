Ferry Building

Pier One Embarcadero, is a terminal for ferries that travel across the San Francisco Bay. At its opening in 1898 it was the largest project undertaken in the city up to that time. Auto ferries were built with an unobstructed lower deck specifically intended for automobiles, and an upper deck for passengers in 1909.

The Former Residence of Charles Tinsley

1247 ½ Webster St. according to the Crocker-Langley City Directory for 1912 (between Ellis and Eddy Streets). Today, this residence has been replaced by apartments. However, according to a San Francisco Bahá’í community list, Charles Tinsley’s address was 1469 Geary.
